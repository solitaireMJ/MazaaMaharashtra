TADOBA-ANDHARI
TIGER RESERVE

Tadoba-Andhari Tiger Reserve, is a prominent wildlife sanctuary located in the Chandrapur district of Maharashtra, India. Established in 1995, the reserve spans an area of approximately 1,727 square kilometers and is named after the Tadoba and Andhari rivers that flow through its territory. This reserve is famous for its rich biodiversity and serves as a crucial habitat for various wildlife species, including the majestic Bengal tiger, leopards, sloth bears, Indian bison (gaurs), wild dogs (dholes), deer species, and numerous bird species. The Tadoba Jungle's landscape is diverse, featuring dense forests, grasslands, and bamboo groves, providing a variety of habitats for the resident wildlife. The reserve has become a popular destination for tourists and wildlife enthusiasts, offering guided safari tours that allow visitors to experience and appreciate the abundant wildlife and natural beauty of the area.

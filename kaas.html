FOREST

The Kaas Pathar, also known as the Kaas Plateau, is a mesmerizing UNESCO World Natural Heritage Site nestled in the Satara district of Maharashtra, India. This enchanting destination is situated approximately 25 kilometers west of Satara city and is a part of the Western Ghats, a UNESCO World Heritage Site celebrated for its incredible biodiversity. The plateau is particularly renowned for its spectacular seasonal flower bloom, which occurs during the monsoon months of August and September. As the rains arrive, the plateau transforms into a vibrant tapestry of colors, with a dazzling array of wildflowers in shades of pink, purple, yellow, and white. Among the many blooms, the delicate and tiny blue "Kaas Plateau Flower" (Cynotis Tuberosa) stands out, being endemic to this region. Beyond its floral beauty, Kas Pathar is also an essential conservation area, harboring numerous endemic and endangered species of plants, insects, and other small creatures.

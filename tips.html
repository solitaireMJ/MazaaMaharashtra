Travel safety-:

1. Try to rely more on credit cards and travellers cheques than cash..

2. As a general rule, city streets that include children and women suggest the area is safe for families.



Transport safety-:

1. At the airport, watch for your suitcase as it appears on the carousel. Don't hang back and wait for the crowds to disperse - you might find that someone else has already taken your bag in the meantime.

2. Carjacking is a problem in some cities. When driving, keep all doors locked and windows up. Make sure your boot is locked too.



Hotel safety-:

1. If possible, choose accommodation that has unmarked 'swipe cards' rather than numbered keys for each room. If you lose your swipe card or if it is stolen, the thief won't know which room to rob.

2. Always lock your hotel door when retiring for the night. If there is a chain included, use it.
